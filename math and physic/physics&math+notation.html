Math and Physics Notations by radin mo

This page lists mathematical and physical notations, organized by topic, from basic to advanced. (examples for each one will be added in future)

1. Basic Arithmetic and Algebraic Notations

  • \( + \): Addition
  • \( - \): Subtraction
  • \( \times \) or \( * \): Multiplication
  • \( \div \) or \( / \): Division
  • \( = \): Equal to
  • \( \neq \): Not equal to
  • \( < \), \( > \): Less than, greater than
  • \( \leq \), \( \geq \): Less than or equal to, greater than or equal to
  • \( \pm \): Plus or minus
  • \( \% \): Percentage
  • \( |x| \): Absolute value of \(x\)
  • \( \sqrt{x} \): Square root of \(x\)
  • \( x^n \): \(x\) raised to the power of \(n\)
  • \( n! \): Factorial of \(n\)

2. Set Theory

  • \( x \in A \): \(x\) is an element of set \(A\)
  • \( x \notin A \): \(x\) is not an element of set \(A\)
  • \( A \subseteq B \): \(A\) is a subset of \(B\)
  • \( A \subset B \): \(A\) is a proper subset of \(B\)
  • \( A \cup B \): Union of sets \(A\) and \(B\)
  • \( A \cap B \): Intersection of sets \(A\) and \(B\)
  • \( \emptyset \): Empty set
  • \( \mathbb{R}, \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{N}, \mathbb{Q}, \mathbb{C} \): Sets of real numbers, integers, natural numbers, rational numbers, and complex numbers, respectively.

3. Functions and Calculus Notations

  • \( f(x) \): Function of \(x\)
  • \( f'(x) \): Derivative of \(f(x)\) with respect to \(x\)
  • \( \frac{dy}{dx} \): Derivative of \(y\) with respect to \(x\)
  • \( \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} \): Second derivative of \(y\) with respect to \(x\)
  • \( \int f(x) \, dx \): Indefinite integral of \(f(x)\)
  • \( \int_a^b f(x) \, dx \): Definite integral from \(a\) to \(b\)
  • \( \lim_{x \to c} f(x) \): Limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x\) approaches \(c\)
  • \( \infty \): Infinity
  • \( \partial f / \partial x \): Partial derivative of \(f\) with respect to \(x\)
  • \( \nabla f \): Gradient of \(f\)
  • \( \nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} \): Divergence of vector field \(\mathbf{F}\)
  • \( \nabla \times \mathbf{F} \): Curl of vector field \(\mathbf{F}\)

4. Linear Algebra Notations

  • \( \mathbf{A}, \mathbf{B} \): Matrices
  • \( \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w} \): Vectors
  • \( \mathbf{I} \): Identity matrix
  • \( \det(\mathbf{A}) \): Determinant of matrix \(\mathbf{A}\)
  • \( \mathbf{A}^{-1} \): Inverse of matrix \(\mathbf{A}\)
  • \( \mathbf{A}^\top \): Transpose of matrix \(\mathbf{A}\)
  • \( \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{v} \): Matrix-vector multiplication
  • \( \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w} \): Cross product of vectors \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\)
  • \( \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{w} \): Dot product of vectors \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\)

5. Probability and Statistics

  • \( P(A) \): Probability of event \(A\)
  • \( E(X) \): Expected value of random variable \(X\)
  • \( \text{Var}(X) \): Variance of \(X\)
  • \( \sigma_X^2 \): Variance of \(X\)
  • \( \sigma_X \): Standard deviation of \(X\)
  • \( P(A | B) \): Conditional probability of \(A\) given \(B\)
  • \( X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2) \): \(X\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\)
  • \( \mathbb{E} \): Expectation
  • \( \text{Cov}(X, Y) \): Covariance of \(X\) and \(Y\)

6. Complex Numbers

  • \( i \): Imaginary unit, where \(i^2 = -1\)
  • \( z = x + iy \): Complex number with real part \(x\) and imaginary part \(y\)
  • \( |z| \): Modulus of \(z\), or \(\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}\)
  • \( \arg(z) \): Argument of \(z\), or the angle \(z\) makes with the positive real axis
  • \( \bar{z} \): Conjugate of \(z = x + iy\), defined as \(\bar{z} = x - iy\)

7. Physics Notations

Mechanics

  • \( m \): Mass
  • \( v \): Velocity
  • \( a \): Acceleration
  • \( F \): Force
  • \( p = mv \): Momentum
  • \( E \): Energy
  • \( W = Fd \): Work (Force times distance)
  • \( P = \frac{W}{t} \): Power (Work per unit time)
  • \( \tau = r \times F \): Torque
  • \( I = \sum m r^2 \): Moment of inertia
  • \( KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^2 \): Kinetic energy
  • \( PE = mgh \): Potential energy

Electricity and Magnetism

  • \( q \): Electric charge
  • \( E \): Electric field
  • \( V \): Electric potential
  • \( I \): Electric current
  • \( R \): Resistance
  • \( C \): Capacitance
  • \( \Phi_B \): Magnetic flux
  • \( B \): Magnetic field
  • \( \mathbf{E} = - \nabla V \): Relation between electric field and potential
  • \( F = qE \): Force on a charge in an electric field
  • \( F = q(v \times B) \): Lorentz force on a moving charge in a magnetic field
  • \( V = IR \): Ohm’s law (Voltage = Current \(\times\) Resistance)

Wave and Optics

  • \( \lambda \): Wavelength
  • \( f \): Frequency
  • \( T \): Period
  • \( v = f \lambda \): Wave velocity
  • \( \theta \): Angle
  • \( n \): Refractive index
  • \( I \): Intensity

Thermodynamics

  • \( Q \): Heat
  • \( W \): Work done by or on a system
  • \( U \): Internal energy
  • \( T \): Temperature
  • \( S \): Entropy
  • \( \Delta U = Q - W \): First law of thermodynamics
  • \( PV = nRT \): Ideal gas law

Quantum Mechanics

  • \( \hbar \): Reduced Planck's constant
  • \( \psi(x, t) \): Wave function
  • \( E = h f \): Energy of a photon (Planck's relation)
  • \( \Delta x \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} \): Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle
  • \( H \psi = E \psi \): Schrödinger’s equation
  • \( \langle x \rangle \): Expectation value of position

8. Advanced Mathematics

Topology

  • \( \mathbb{R}^n \): \(n\)-dimensional Euclidean space
  • \( \subseteq \): Subset
  • \( \cup \): Union of sets
  • \( \cap \): Intersection of sets
  • \( \partial A \): Boundary of set \(A\)

Differential Geometry

  • \( g_{\mu\nu} \): Metric tensor
  • \( R_{\mu\nu} \): Ricci tensor
  • \( R \): Ricci scalar
  • \( T_{\mu\nu} \): Energy-momentum tensor

Tensor Notation

  • \( A_{ij} \): Component of a tensor
  • \( \delta_{ij} \): Kronecker delta (identity tensor)
  • \( \epsilon_{ijk} \): Levi-Civita symbol (antisymmetric tensor)

9. Advanced Physics

Relativity

  • \( E = mc^2 \): Energy-mass equivalence
  • \( g_{\mu\nu} \): Metric tensor in general relativity
  • \( c \): Speed of light
  • \( \gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \): Lorentz factor

Quantum Field Theory

  • \( \phi \): Scalar field
  • \( \mathcal{L} \): Lagrangian density
  • \( \mathcal{H} \): Hamiltonian density
  • \( \partial_{\mu} \): Partial derivative with respect to spacetime coordinates
  • \( \hat{a}^\dagger, \hat{a} \): Creation and annihilation operators
